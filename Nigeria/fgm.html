Female Genital Mutilation

14% of women age 15-49 have undergone FGM. The practice varies dramatically by region, from 1% in some northern states to 50% in Katsina. 71% of women believe it should end.

Early Childhood Practice

85% of circumcised women were cut before age 5, with most performed in infancy. This makes prevention efforts critical in the earliest years of life.

Who performs FGM

95% of girls are cut by traditional agents, while only 5% by medical professionals. For older women, 82% were cut by traditional agents.

Shifting attitudes

71% of women and 74% of men believe FGM should not be continued. Only 16-20% believe it is required by their religion.
