The Skilled Birth Gap

Less than half of Nigerian births are attended by a doctor, nurse, or midwife. Nearly a third of women deliver with only traditional attendants, relatives, or no one at all.

The wealth divide

92% of births among the wealthiest families have skilled attendance, compared to just 16% among the poorest — a 6x gap.

Education matters even more

Women with more than secondary education have 91% skilled delivery, vs 19% for women with no education — nearly 5x higher.
