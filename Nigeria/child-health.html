The Dropout Problem

While 65% of children receive their first dose of oral polio vaccine, only 20% complete all vaccinations. High dropout rates between doses signal problems with follow-up care and access.

Zero-dose children persist

31% of children age 12-23 months have received no vaccinations at all — a critical gap that puts them at severe risk.

Education drives coverage

Children of mothers with higher education have 43% full vaccination coverage, compared to just 10% for children of uneducated mothers.
